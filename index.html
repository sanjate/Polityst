About

Hi, I'm Sanja.

I'm a policy analyst and systems thinker working at the intersection of policy, strategy, and social impact.

I write and advise on how systems function in practice: why well-meant initiatives often fail to scale, why misaligned incentives undermine impact, and how institutional choices shape what becomes possible. This work ranges from analytical writing and policy briefs to strategic advising and capacity-building initiatives.

Alongside system-level policy work, I collaborate with scientists, founders, and civil society organisations who want to engage with society beyond their immediate domain. Many care deeply about contributing to the public good, but find themselves navigating structures poorly suited to that aim. I help them make sense of those constraints — and design more effective ways of acting within them.

Across all of this, my underlying focus is impact capacity: the ability to understand complex systems, make informed strategic choices, and act with durability over time. Impact here is not about optics or moral signalling, but about whether ideas, organisations, and policies are designed to hold up under real-world conditions.

If this way of thinking resonates, you're welcome to start a conversation.
